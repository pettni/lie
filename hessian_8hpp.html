Compute approximate Hessian (second derivative) of the squared norm of rminus.

The computed Hessian is

\[ \mathrm{d}^{2r} \left( \frac{1}{2} \| x \ominus_r y \|^2 \right)_{xx}. \]

Note
The first derivative is

\[ \mathrm{d}^r \left( \frac{1}{2} \| x \ominus_r y \|^2 \right)_x = a^T \mathrm{d}^r \exp_a^{-1}, \]

where \(a = x \ominus_r y\), so the Hessian can be equivalently expressed as the the first-order derivative

\[ \mathrm{d}^r \left( a^T \mathrm{d}^r \exp_a^{-1} \right)_x, \]

The Hessian can be expressed as the infinite sum

\[ \left( \sum_n B_n \frac{(-1)^n}{n!} \mathrm{d}^r \left( (ad_a^T)^n a \right)_a \right) \mathrm{d}^r \exp_a^{-1}. \]

This function computes a finite number Terms of terms by recursively computing the inner derivatives.
